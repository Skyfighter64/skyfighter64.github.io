# KaTeX Test Page

This page demonstrates **inline** and **block** math with KaTeX.

---

### Inline Math

The famous equation: $E = mc^2$ appears inline within text.  
Another inline example: $\alpha + \beta = \gamma$.

---

### Display Math (Block)

Here’s a centered block equation:

$$
\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \, dx = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}
$$

Another one with summation:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}
$$

---

### Using Alternative Delimiters

Parenthesis style:  
\(\frac{a}{b} = \frac{1}{2}\)

Brackets style:  
\[
\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}
\]
